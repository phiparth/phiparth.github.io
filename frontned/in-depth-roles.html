🎤 In-Depth Speaker Role Analysis

(Asian Parliamentary Format)

Each side has 3 speakers:
• Government (supports motion)
• Opposition (opposes motion)
Each speaker speaks for 7 minutes.

✅ 1. Prime Minister (PM)

Burden: Set up the debate and lay the foundation for the Government case.

Key Duties:
• Define the motion (especially for abstract or moral motions)
• Set the framing and scope of the debate
• Introduce 2–3 core arguments for the Government side
• Explain why this debate matters (impact)

Should not:
• Leave definitions vague or overly “squirrel” the motion
• Skip burdens or assume points are obvious

Example Motion: This House would ban zoos
→ PM must prove that banning zoos benefits animals and society more than keeping them.

❌ 2. Leader of Opposition (LO)

Burden: Clash with PM and build the Opposition’s case.

Key Duties:
• Directly rebut Government arguments
• Propose alternative framing (if needed)
• Present 2–3 Opposition arguments
• Clearly state Opp side’s burden (e.g., prove reform > ban)

Should not:
• Only rebut without building a positive case
• Agree with Gov’s framing without challenge (if it's flawed)

✅ 3. Deputy Prime Minister (DPM)

Burden: Defend Government case and maintain momentum.

Key Duties:
• Rebut LO’s arguments
• Defend PM’s points with deeper analysis
• Add 1 new argument (if needed)
• Refine framing or clarify definitions if LO challenged them

Should not:
• Just repeat PM’s content
• Bring in too much unrelated new material

❌ 4. Deputy Leader of Opposition (DLO)

Burden: Defend Opposition case and rebuild after Gov’s attacks.

Key Duties:
• Rebut DPM
• Strengthen LO’s points with new layers
• Add 1 new argument (if needed)
• Deepen clash and reinforce Opp framing

Should not:
• Spend too much time on one rebuttal
• Ignore Gov framing if it's dominating the debate

✅ 5. Government Whip (GW)

Burden: Summarize the entire debate and explain why Gov wins.

Key Duties:
• No new arguments
• Rebut DLO
• Compare both teams: "Here’s the clash → here’s who did better"
• Weigh impacts: why Gov side matters more (morally, practically, etc.)
• Frame how the judge should evaluate the round

Should not:
• Introduce new content
• Merely restate previous points — must evaluate the debate

Pro Tip: Think like a mini-judge: explain the story of the round from your side’s perspective.

❌ 6. Opposition Whip (OW)

Burden: Close the debate from Opp’s side and secure the win.

Key Duties:
• No new arguments
• Rebut GW
• Explain the big picture: “Here’s why Opp’s case was stronger”
• Weigh both sides: “Even if their point is true, ours is more important/more likely/etc.”
• Lock in judge’s perspective on Opp’s framing and burdens

Should not:
• Be reactive only — must shape a final Opp narrative
• Repeat LO/DLO without analysis

🔁 Summary Table: Speaker Burdens

Speaker Role Summary Core Burdens
PM Opens Gov case Define, Frame, Give Arguments
LO Opens Opp case Rebut PM, Give Opp Case
DPM Defends Gov Rebut LO, Deepen PM’s case
DLO Defends Opp Rebut DPM, Deepen LO’s case
GW Closes Gov No new arguments, Frame why Gov wins
OW Closes Opp No new arguments, Frame why Opp wins

🎯 Final Tips on Role Mastery

• Whips win debates. If both teams make good arguments, the whip that explains better usually takes the round.
• Don’t just speak — debate. Clash, weigh, and impact everything.
• Roles are not just labels. Each has a distinct burden — mastering that makes you a strategist, not just a speaker.

🌀 Reply Speeches in Asian Parliamentary (AP) Format

🧾 Is a reply speech part of regular AP?
In standard AP format, reply speeches are optional and only included if the tournament rules specify it.

⏳ Basic Rules (when included):
• Each team gives one reply speech.
• Gov reply is first, Opp reply is last.
• 4 minutes long (shorter than regular speeches).
• Given by either the 1st or 2nd speaker (never the whip).
• No new arguments allowed.

🎯 Purpose of a Reply Speech:
The reply speech is like a “biased judge summary.”
You are reviewing the debate from your team’s side, showing why you should win.

📌 What You Do in a Reply Speech:
1. Summarize the major clashes
2. Weigh impacts
3. Highlight your team’s strategy
4. Deliver a clear narrative

🚫 What You Cannot Do:
• Introduce new arguments
• Act like another whip (no rehashing every point)
• Just list your team’s arguments — you must compare and weigh
